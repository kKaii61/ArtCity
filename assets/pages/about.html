ABOUT ARTCITY

SOCIAL ART PLATFORM

Join in, it's FREE

An open platform for art-lovers, artists, galleries, collectors, universities and anyone who is involved in the art world in some way.

Create your own profile, make connections, followers, post on your wall and much more.

A Theme-Based Social Network

Imagine a LinkedIn for art, as social as Facebook, plus a shop and plenty of entertaining and informative content.

Served from Germany.

Popular worldwide.

Connecting The Art World

Members, Artists, Galleries and Universities from +200 countries ... and counting.

ARTCITY’s Shop is a Marketplace

Seller and buyer acting directly, similar as you might know it from eBay for example.

We're re-writing the rules; democratising the art-online-shop world.

ARTCITY is the world’s first direct and secondary marketplace for art!

Next to established artists, this is opening the doors for undiscovered talents all over the world, being able to display and sell their art and earn what they deserve.

Moreover, also private owners of artworks can have their marketplace on ARTCITY. This is a world’s first since private owners cannot sell there artworks anywhere else, neither online nor offline.

Once you have your ARTCITY profile you can start your Marketplace-Plan. You’ll find the Sell Art – button in the menu. Click on it and follow the steps.

A marketplace subscription plan is available for free. You can upgrade to a Premium-Plan anytime. ARTCITY does not charge sales commission; only a payment gateway fee of 2.5%. (*Additional PayPal or Credit Card fees may apply).

Collector's Benefits

The real price for a contemporary artwork was never reflecting the real value, because extraordinary high gallery and online-shop commissions blow up sales prices.

Therefore, purchasing an artwork on ARTCITY will give you the confidence, that you have paid what the artists will gain from the sale.

This will give you the chance to even sell contemporary artworks without having to wait until a potential price increase goes beyond 30+%, which is usually the minimum commission paid with the price in the first place.
